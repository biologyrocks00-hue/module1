Module 2 - The Cell Theory

Introduction: What is a Cell?

Cells are the basic building blocks of all living things. From the smallest bacteria to a giant blue whale, every organism is made of cells. In fact, your own body is composed of approximately 37 trillion cells!

Think of cells as tiny "factories" that:

Provide Structure as they give your body its shape and support.

Generate Energy as they take in nutrients from food and convert them into energy (ATP).

Store Information since they contain your DNA, the "blueprints" for everything you are.

Reproduce as they can make exact copies of themselves to help you grow or heal.

The Three Postulates of Cell Theory

The Cell Theory is one of the most important foundations of modern biology. It is based on three main principles (postulates) developed by scientists like Matthias Schleiden, Theodor Schwann, and Rudolf Virchow.

Postulate Meaning in Simple Terms
1. All living things are composed of one or more cells. Whether it is a single-celled amoeba or a multi-celled human, if it‚Äôs alive, it‚Äôs made of cells.
2. The cell is the basic unit of life. The cell is the smallest structure that can perform all the functions of life independently.
3. All cells arise from pre-existing cells. Cells do not appear out of nowhere; they are created when a "parent" cell divides into two "daughter" cells.

Quick Study Tip

These three principles were developed in the mid-19th century by scientists Matthias Schleiden, Theodor Schwann, and Rudolf Virchow. Together, they form the foundation of modern biology!
